Founder’s Note

ElevateX didn’t begin as a large organization. It began as a simple frustration.

I kept seeing student-led initiatives with strong intent but no continuity. There was energy, there were ideas — but most of it stayed temporary. A few events, a few drives, and then it faded. There was no system to sustain or scale what was being built.

ElevateX was built to change that.

When Samridh, Devyansh and I began working on this, we didn’t expect it to become what it is today. On 8th March, when ElevateX officially came into existence, it was still just an idea we were trying to make work — nothing more.

From the beginning, the focus was clear. Not on doing more, but on building something that could last.

Structure was one part of that. Systems, roles, processes — things that allow work to continue without depending on a few people.

But structure alone doesn’t build an organization.

What truly defines ElevateX is its culture.

The late-night meetings after work was done. The conversations that had nothing to do with tasks. The inside jokes, the stickers, the countless WhatsApp groups that made this feel less like an obligation and more like a space people wanted to be in.

Those small, almost insignificant moments — they are what built this.

Because when people feel like they belong, they stay. And when they stay, they build.

There were moments where this could have fallen apart.

An internal conflict that could have broken the organization entirely. A night before an event where the venue was cancelled last minute, and everything we had worked for was at risk.

But it didn’t collapse.

People stepped in. Not because they had to — but because they felt responsible for what this was.

That is the difference.

Most student organizations don’t fail because of lack of ideas. They fail because there is nothing that holds people together.

ElevateX works because people don’t feel like they are working for it. They feel like it belongs to them.

From the start, the approach was simple — give individuals ownership. Not in theory, but in practice. This is your organization. Build what you want to build. Take responsibility for it.

That is what scaled ElevateX.

Not just events or initiatives, but people who were willing to take ownership and carry things forward.

For me, ElevateX has been more than just an organization. It has completely changed how I think, how I work, and who I am.

And that is what I want this to become for others.

Not just a place to participate, but a place that changes people — in ways that stay long after they leave.

This is still a work in progress.

And that is exactly how it should be.

— Vivaan Agarwal
Founder, ElevateX

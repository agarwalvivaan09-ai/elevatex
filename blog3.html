Why Financial Literacy Matters

Financial literacy is one of the most practical skills a student can develop, yet it remains largely absent from formal education. Students are taught complex theories, but rarely how to manage money, assess risk, or make informed financial decisions.

This gap becomes visible very quickly. People enter adulthood without understanding saving, investing, or even basic financial planning. The consequences aren’t immediate—but they compound over time.

The issue isn’t lack of intelligence. It’s lack of exposure. Financial concepts are often seen as advanced or optional, when in reality they are foundational to everyday life.

Project Unnati was created to address this gap—not through traditional teaching, but through practical learning. The goal was to make financial concepts accessible, relevant, and directly applicable.

Instead of focusing on theory, the emphasis was on understanding—how money works, how decisions affect outcomes, and how small actions compound over time.

Financial awareness changes behavior. And when introduced early, it creates long-term impact that extends far beyond academics.
